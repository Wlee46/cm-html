Dear Sydney Buskers

In light of news coverage and posts on social media over the last few days, we wanted to get in touch to address your concerns and clarify details about our updated Sydney Busking Code.

It's important to note that we have not 'banned' busking in the city, but in the interest of safety and shared use of space, we have introduced designated areas for performances on George Street. We are currently reviewing these designations to see whether we can increase the number of busking opportunities along the light rail corridor, but this may not be possible given safety concerns and the needs of the broader community.

We apologise that our recent emails -- first encouraging you to provide feedback on the Busking Code, then explaining the changes we had made -- were not as clear as they could have been.

We know there has been some concern about where you can perform and the reasons behind our changes and we hope this email provides some clarity.

Why do we have a Busking Code?

We strongly support buskers and the colour and vibrancy you bring to our city. That's our starting point.

But we can't have a free-for-all because we need to balance the entire community's right to use our public spaces.

That's where the Code comes in. It helps us balance both your needs as performers and the needs of others in the city -- visitors, workers, businesses and residents.

The Code encourages you to share the best spots and limit overlap of performance space, maintain safety (for yourself, your audience and people passing by) and consider the impact, particularly of amplified sound, on local businesses and residents.

While we want to support you to bring life to our streets and make a living, we don't want situations that are unsafe, where you are competing to be heard over each other, or overly dominating spaces that need to be shared.

Updating the Code

From time to time we review and update our busking policy to ensure it is meeting the above purposes.

George Street

Given the introduction of light rail and the increased numbers of pedestrians in the area, not all locations along George Street are safe for buskers to set up. In the past year, we have received hundreds of complaints about buskers performing along George Street.

So, to get the balance right, we identified six Special Busking Sites along George Street, and one in the QVB forecourt, to protect for performance. We chose sites that are already working well and are safe because they don't encourage passing pedestrians to step in front of the light rail.

We ask all artists to stick to these sites. Specific locations and maps can be found in the Sydney Busking Code.

Pitt Street Mall

In Pitt Street Mall we have had three designated areas for many years. The same buskers have been able to use the same sites for a very long time, so we are trying a new configuration that will allow a range of buskers access, and a change of experience for the businesses directly adjacent.

Musicians will have access to the central pitch (outside the Mid City Centre). Extended duration performers, such as pavement and performance artists, will have access to the areas just north and south (underneath the skybridge, and outside the Strand Arcade).

There is another dedicated pitch for musicians slightly further north on Martin Place at Pitt Street.

Consultation

These updates were placed on public exhibition between 24 June and 5 August. Public exhibition is a legal term for the requirement of Councils to publish proposed changes online and ask the community for feedback.

All registered buskers who have provided the City of Sydney with an email address were notified of this process and encouraged to leave feedback on the proposed changes.

We received just four submissions from buskers, none of which raised concerns about the proposed changes to George Street.

But we understand not everyone understood this process and we have heard a greater range of views over the past few days. We want you to know we have heard you and respect your opinion.

What next?

The Lord Mayor has asked City of Sydney staff to review the George Street designations with a view to increasing the number of busking opportunities.

This may or may not be possible. We continue to share the safety concerns raised by the light rail operator, and still have to consider the needs of the broader community and everyone's right to accessing our public walkways.

Buskers are ambassadors for the cultural life of Sydney and values we celebrate: diversity, respect and inclusion.

Your views are important to us and we want to work with you to ensure we get a configuration that balances public safety and harmonious shared used of the spaces.
